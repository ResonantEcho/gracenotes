A Statement of Faith

Gratia et Cantus Firmus

My faith is not a list of rigid rules, but a living melody built upon the unwavering anchor of God's grace in Jesus Christ.

I. God: The Architect
I believe in a God of radical, boundless grace. As the "Repairer of Broken Walls," God works constantly to restore humanity through unconditional, inclusive love.
"The Lord is merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love." — Psalm 103:8
II. Jesus: The Christ
Jesus is the visible image of the invisible God. He dismantled barriers of exclusion and defeated the powers of sin to offer salvation and a life of humble service.
"He is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation." — Colossians 1:15
III. The Holy Spirit: The Animating Breath
The Spirit is the Parakletos — the Divine Advocate who empowers us and intercedes for us. The greatest sign of the Spirit's presence is Love.
"The Spirit helps us in our weakness... intercedes with sighs too deep for words." — Romans 8:26
IV. Humanity: Saints and Sinners
Created in the Image of God yet profoundly broken, we are simultaneously saints and sinners. We are entirely dependent on divine grace for restoration.
"For by grace you have been saved through faith... it is the gift of God." — Ephesians 2:8–9
V. Prayer: The Essential Dialogue
Prayer is the essential breath of the Christian life. It is a posture of listening and communion that aligns our hearts with God's heart.
"Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances." — 1 Thess 5:16–18
VI. Scripture: The Score
The Bible is the inspired witness to the Word of God. Through a Christocentric lens, we find it a living guide for a relevant word in today's world.
"Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path." — Psalm 119:105
VII. Worship: Orienting Life
Worship is the act of orienting our lives toward God, synchronizing our personal rhythms back to the cantus firmus of grace.
"True worshipers will worship the Father in Spirit and truth." — John 4:23
VIII. Baptism: Rebirth
An outward symbol of dying and rising with Christ. It marks our adoption into God's family, empowered for witness and service.
"We were buried with him by baptism... so we too might walk in newness of life." — Romans 6:4
IX. Communion: The Lord's Table
Through anamnesis, we enter the saving mystery of Christ here and now. This table is a place of radical hospitality where the broken are made whole.
"Do this in remembrance of me." — Luke 22:19
X. Leadership: Three Strands
A cord of three strands: Proclamation, Service, and Oversight. Leadership is about modeling the radical humility and service of Christ.
"The Son of Man came not to be served but to serve." — Mark 10:45
XI. The Church: Unity in Diversity
The Church is the living Body of Christ. Unity is not uniformity, but the harmony of different voices singing the same song of Love.
"Making every effort to maintain the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace." — Ephesians 4:3
